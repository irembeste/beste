How can we make IoT home appliances more trustworthy?

IoT Devices On Rise

But how secure are they?

Meet SmartGuard

  Global standard for certifying IoT home device security

Currently, there isn't any global consensus regarding device security standards. Even national regulations tend to only cover devices to be used by the government and not the consumer. Our solution helps to set up security standards that would be comprehensive and recognised all around the world.

  Increased Baseline Security

Device makers aren't prioritising security. Plenty of common exploits that are being used by hackers can be easily prevented. We want all devices to address these vulnerabilities as a bare minimum to having a secure device.

  Generate Public Awareness

Consumers aren't conscious about security while looking to purchase IoT devices. Our branding helps them identify secure devices, aiding them in their decision while purchasing. This also encourages customers to give more thought to security and understand more about the risks posed by vulnerable devices.

FAQ

How does it work?

Each time a device manufacturer releases a new device, they will send the device to us to verify the security of the device. we will perform security tests against all currently known exploits. The list of these will be expanded as we discover more vulnerabilities. For instance, we could try to use default usernames and passwords to gain access to the device.
Once the device demonstrates that it adequately addresses these vulnerabilities, we will certify the device as secured using our brand symbol. Otherwise, the device will be returned to the manufacturer and will be retested once they have addressed the issue.

Why would the companies come to us instead of doing the tests themselves?

Most of the company claims that their devices is secure but they get hacked over time because of simple mistakes. So even though they ask everyone to trust them, it still does not prove their strength in security. So if there is a global authority verifying the security of their devices, it will be easier for them to detech these easy mistakes before it goes to the market. Most of the small or medium company does not run security tests on their devices as general people prioratises features over security. But because of the recent hack, a lot of companies has lost of a big chunk of revenue. So they will be happy if a certified authority is verifying the security.
